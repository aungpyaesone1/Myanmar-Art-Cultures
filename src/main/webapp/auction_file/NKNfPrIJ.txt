<p><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">The nats&nbsp;</span><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">are spirits worshipped in Myanmar&nbsp;</span><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">in conjunction with Buddhism</span><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">. They are divided between the 37 Great Nats and all the rest . Almost all of the 37 Great Nats were human beings who met violent death</span><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">. They may thus also be called&nbsp;</span><em style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">nat sein</em><span style="color: #222222; font-family: sans-serif;">. The word 'sein', while meaning 'green', is being used to mean 'raw' in this context. There are however two types of nats in Burmese Buddhist belief.</span></p>
